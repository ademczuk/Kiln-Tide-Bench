A two-sister studio working slow batches of stoneware out of a clapboard barn fifty yards above the high-water line. We pull our clay bodies from kilns that breathe with the weather, and we glaze in the colors we wake up to.

Est. 2019 Open studio Saturdays 10–2 Salt-fire kiln · spring & autumn

The autumn salt-fire

Our wood-and-salt kiln only fires twice a year. Pieces emerge with a flashed orange-peel skin no electric kiln can imitate.

Reserved pots ship the week the kiln cools. We post the unloading on the studio journal each season.

Two sisters, one barn, one tide table.

We grew up running the dock road in summer, learning that the working coast and the postcard coast are the same coast at different hours. After ten years apart — June throwing in Portland, Lila glazing in Bristol — we came home in 2019 and rebuilt our grandfather's lobster-gear barn into a studio.

The tide tells you when to fire and when to walk away. We try to listen to both.

We work in small batches because the salt kiln only opens twice a year, and because we still answer every email ourselves. Pieces are sold first to the people on our studio list, then through the door on Saturdays, then online if anything is left.

— June & Lila Thatcher, Boothbay Harbor
