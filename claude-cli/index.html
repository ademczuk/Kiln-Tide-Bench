The Studio

A kiln on a granite ledge, and the schedule of the bay.

Kiln & Tide began in 2019 in a cedar-shingle outbuilding behind our grandfather's house in Stonington. Margaret had quit a graphic design job in Brooklyn; June was teaching high-school art and throwing on weekends. We bought a used Skutt kiln off a retiring potter in Camden and drove it home through fog so thick we missed the turn twice.

We work to the rhythm of the bay. Spring through autumn we throw, trim, glaze, and fire. Winters we go quiet, mend nets of old work, plan the next year's forms. Every piece passes through both our hands at least once, which is also the reason we'll never scale.

— Margaret & June Halloran, founders

The Letter

A short note when the kiln cools.

Three or four times a year, when a firing comes out of the kiln, we send a letter: what was made, what survived, and where to find it. No urgency, no scarcity tricks — just first dibs for people who care to read.
