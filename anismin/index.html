The Sisters

We grew up digging clay from the riverbank.

Our grandmother taught us to throw on a kickwheel in her kitchen in Brooklin. Wren stayed in Maine after college; June came back from a decade in Portland, Oregon, with a head full of glaze chemistry and an opinion about every kiln she'd ever loaded.

We share the boatshed with two retired lobster traps, a woodstove older than either of us, and a kiln we named Margaret. Everything you see here was made within sight of the water.
