The studio

Two sisters, one old kiln, and a lot of weather.

Kiln & Tide began when Mara came home to Maine after a decade in restaurant kitchens and June finally admitted that the “temporary” wheel in the shed had become a second full-time job. They converted their grandfather’s bait room into a bright little studio, patched the floorboards, and started firing small weekend batches between lobster boats and rain squalls.

Their work is rooted in utility, not perfection. Handles are meant to feel good in a cold hand. Bowls should stack well, pour well, and survive ordinary mornings. The glazes borrow from the coast around them: eelgrass green, fog blue, mussel-shell gray, weathered brick.

Nothing here is factory smooth, and that is the charm. Each piece carries a little evidence of the hand that made it.

Where we make it Cushing, Maine, in a one-room studio a few minutes from the water.
How we fire Small electric kiln runs, twice monthly, with seasonal glaze tests in between.
What we value Useful forms, honest materials, and the kind of objects that get better with regular use.
What changes Shapes stay familiar, but no two glaze runs ever land exactly the same.
